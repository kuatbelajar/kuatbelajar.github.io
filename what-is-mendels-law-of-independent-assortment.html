3. what is universal law of gravitation?​


Jawaban:

Objects in the Universe attract each other

Penjelasan dengan langkah-langkah:

Newton's law of universal gravitation states that objects in the universe attract each other with a force that is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them. (Separately showed that mass is spherical symmetrically attracted as if all mass were concentrated in their centers.) This is a general law of physics derived from an empirical observation that Isaac Newton called induction.[1] it is part of classical mechanics and was formulated in Newton's Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica ("the Principia"), first published on July 5, 1687. (When Newton's book was presented in 1686 to the Royal Society, Robert Hooke claimed that Newton obtained the inverse of the quadratic law. of it.) In modern parlance, this law states that:

Each point mass attracts every other point mass by a force along the intersection of the two points. The force is directly proportional to the product of the two masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them:

{\displaystyle F=G{\frac {m_{1}m_{2}}{r^{2}}}\ }{\displaystyle F=G{\frac {m_{1}m_{2}}{r ^{2}}}\ },

where:

F is the force between the masses,

G is the gravitational constant,

m1 is the mass of the first object,

m2 is the mass of the second object, and

r is the distance between the center of the object.

Diagram of two masses attracting one another

Using SI units, F is measured in newtons (N), m1 and m2 in kilograms (kg), r in meters (m), and the constant G is approximately equal to 6.674×10−11 N m2 kg−2. The value of the constant G was first accurately determined from the Cavendish experiment by the English scientist Henry Cavendish in 1798, although Cavendish did not calculate a numerical value for G.[2] This research was also the first test of Newton's theory of gravity between masses in the laboratory. It was 111 years after the publication of Newton's book "Principia" and 71 years after Newton's death, so none of Newton's formulas used the value of G; instead he could only calculate strength relative to other forces.

Newton's law of gravity resembles Coulomb's law of electric strength, which is used to calculate the magnitude of the electric force between two charged objects. Both are inverse-squares laws, where the force is inversely proportional to the square of the distance between objects. Coulomb's law has the product of two charges on the product of mass, and the electrostatic constant on the gravitational constant.

Newton's laws have been superseded by Einstein's general theory of relativity, but continue to be used as an excellent approximation of the effects of gravity. Relativity is necessary only when there is a need for extreme precision, or when dealing with very strong gravitational fields, such as those found in very large and dense bodies, or at very close distances (such as Mercury's orbit around the sun)

It is a specialized language used in the legal field that has its own vocabulary, grammar, and style. Legal English can differ from regular English in several ways, including the use of archaic or Latin terms, technical terminology, and formal language.

One of the main differences between legal and regular English is the use of precise and concise language in legal documents. Legal documents must be written in a clear and precise manner to avoid ambiguity and to ensure that all parties understand the legal terms and conditions.

Legal English is essential for law students because they need to be able to understand and communicate legal concepts and ideas accurately and effectively. Law students must be able to read and interpret legal documents and cases and must be able to write legal documents, such as contracts and briefs, in a clear and concise manner.

Moreover, the use of legal English is important because legal professionals must be able to communicate with each other, their clients, and the courts effectively. Lawyers and legal professionals need to be able to write legal documents, such as contracts and legal opinions, in a way that is clear and concise and accurately conveys their legal opinions and advice.

In summary, legal English is a specialized language used in legal contexts, and it is essential for law students and legal professionals to be proficient in legal English to effectively communicate in the legal field.

26. berikan pendapat anda tentang supremacy of law, equality before the law dan due process of law​


Pendapat saya tentang supremacy of law (keutamaan hukum), equality before the law (kesetaraan di hadapan hukum), dan due process of law (proses hukum yang wajar) adalah sebagai berikut:

1. Supremacy of Law: Keutamaan hukum adalah prinsip fundamental dalam sistem hukum yang menegaskan bahwa hukum berada di atas semua individu dan institusi, termasuk pemerintah itu sendiri. Artinya, semua orang, termasuk pejabat pemerintah, harus tunduk dan patuh terhadap hukum. Supremasi hukum menciptakan dasar yang kuat untuk menjaga keteraturan, keadilan, dan keamanan dalam suatu masyarakat.

2. Equality before the Law: Kesetaraan di hadapan hukum berarti bahwa setiap individu, tanpa memandang status sosial, kekayaan, ras, agama, atau jenis kelamin, memiliki hak yang sama untuk diakui, dihormati, dan dilindungi oleh hukum. Prinsip ini menjamin bahwa semua orang memiliki hak yang sama untuk memperoleh perlakuan yang adil dan sama dalam sistem peradilan. Tidak ada diskriminasi yang diperbolehkan dalam perlakuan hukum terhadap individu.

3. Due Process of Law: Proses hukum yang wajar (due process of law) adalah prinsip yang menjamin bahwa setiap individu memiliki hak untuk menjalani proses hukum yang adil, terbuka, dan berkeadilan sebelum dijatuhi hukuman atau sanksi. Ini berarti bahwa individu harus diberikan kesempatan untuk menyampaikan pembelaan mereka, menghadiri persidangan yang adil, dan tidak boleh diproses secara sembarangan atau sewenang-wenang oleh pemerintah atau otoritas yang berwenang. Proses hukum yang wajar melibatkan perlindungan hak-hak individu, termasuk hak atas kebebasan berbicara, hak atas pengadilan yang cepat, dan hak atas privasi.

Ketiga prinsip ini adalah prinsip-prinsip penting dalam sistem hukum yang demokratis. Mereka merupakan dasar untuk menciptakan masyarakat yang adil, teratur, dan berkeadilan di mana semua orang diperlakukan sama di bawah hukum. Penerapan dan penghormatan terhadap keutamaan hukum, kesetaraan di hadapan hukum, dan proses hukum yang wajar adalah kunci dalam menjaga integritas sistem peradilan dan melindungi hak-hak individu.

Jawaban:

1. Supremacy of law merujuk pada prinsip bahwa hukum adalah yang tertinggi dalam sistem hukum dan harus ditaati oleh semua orang, termasuk pemerintah. Prinsip ini penting untuk memastikan bahwa tindakan pemerintah dan individu tidak melanggar hak-hak individu dan prinsip-prinsip keadilan.
2. quality before the law adalah prinsip bahwa setiap individu harus diperlakukan secara sama di bawah hukum, tanpa kecuali. Prinsip ini penting untuk memastikan bahwa hukum diterapkan secara adil dan tidak diskriminatif.
3. Due process of law adalah prinsip bahwa setiap individu harus diberikan kesempatan untuk membela diri dan mendapatkan perlindungan hukum yang adil. Prinsip ini penting untuk memastikan bahwa tindakan pemerintah tidak melanggar hak-hak individu dan bahwa setiap orang diperlakukan secara adil dalam sistem peradilan.
